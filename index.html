Once a trigger is created, a store manager will receive an Action - curated by the upper management levels from store data analysis. This allows the store manager to act on immediately to improve store's KPI.

About the company Kepler Analytics, Melbourne Kepler Analytics is an AI assistant for brick-and-mortar retail managers that collects, analyses and recommends actions to:
1. More effectively grow store sales
2. Increase productivity of store operating resources

Retailers using Kepler increase sales, improve customer satisfaction and decrease operating cost across 87% of their store network within a year.
Design Process
Our challenge "How might we help on-site retail store managers to get more sales from a list of possible actions?"
The User Research
Expert Interviews
A/B Testing
User Flows
Key Findings
Insight #1
Through our expert interviews, we found out that there can be multiple store managers assigned to one store. These managers are responsible for the store’s performance.
Implementation: Created User Groups and User hierarchy to assign roles for each user.
Insight #2
Managers who have a stronger sense of accountability for the store’s performance tend to perform better - in terms of Sales, Shopfront conversion, Average Transaction Value etc.
Implementation: Action Escalation will escalate the Action to a higher manager (Area Managers) if Action is not actioned.
Insight #3
Headquarter needs a way to communicate to store managers on Ad-hoc activities that has to be distributed fast, validated ad approved.
Implementation: Ad-Hoc Actions and Coaching Cards allows them to create an action that requires the store manager to read, act on and sign digitally after.

Final Prototype



Key Takeaways Communication between store managers and the headquarter is key to successfully help the managers meet store’s performance targets. It is essential to have a seamless workflow of Action assignment, validation and approval after an Action is done. Hence, it is important to be actively involved in their current systems and workflow (through multiple iterations and user feedbacks), in order to deeply understand the problem.

About the company Kepler Analytics, Melbourne Kepler Analytics is an AI assistant for brick-and-mortar retail managers that collects, analyses and recommends actions to:
1. More effectively grow store sales
2. Increase productivity of store operating resources

Retailers using Kepler increase sales, improve customer satisfaction and decrease operating cost across 87% of their store network within a year.
Design Process
Our challenge "How might we provide useful insights to retail businesses that will help them make impactful decisions?”
The User Research
Expert Interviews
Use Case Studies
User Journeys
Key Findings
Insight #1
Managers are always on-the-go. They want to be able to view and share information from their phone.

Implementation: Created mobile prototype of the dashboard that will show their own store’s real-time updates on the landing page - this is called the CET page.
Insight #2
Clients want to be able to view a different date time range for multiple date granularity of the measures easily and through different scalable charts such as bar and line charts.

Implementation: Dashboard 3.0 allows them to choose -
1) Date time range (e.g. Jan 2020 - June 2020)
2) Date Granularity (e.g. Monthly)
3) Date Grouping (e.g. Day of Week) for flexible data calculation and visual presentations (Table, Line Chart or Bar Chart).
Insight #3
Managers want to control what their store managers for confidentiality reasons and also to make sure that they are aware of their store targets for that day.

Implementation: Dashboard 3.0 allows them to customize dashboards and assign users to the dashboards. Every user has a role that has different security systems in place that restricts them from scaling up to see too much data.

Key Takeaways I learned that there can be many factors that will affect the project roadmap and timeline if it is not discussed in detailed. It is also crucial to stay focused on the human side of the product - this is a challenge that I faced when big data is involved. Overall, the project was a great experience and I learned meaningful lessons and know what mistakes to avoid.

Englify Singapore Management University, Singapore
Englify is my University's Final Year Project mobile application that aims to ensure that the English teachers are fully equipped (listening/speaking) to teach their students according to the Myanmar Government English syllabus. We were sponsored by Adrian Siew - more details of him below - to help him build this application for the Myanmar community. We also worked closely together with english teachers in Myanmar and followed their government's approved education syllabus.

About the Sponsor Adrian Siew, Managing Director of Nacre Holdings Pte Ltd Adrian Siew has interests in the Myanmar community and realized that one of the problems in the education system is their English speaking capability. He came to SMU in search for students who would be willing to help and there he met our Project Manager, Nancy.

Watch our video here:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhGW7jRoqXA
Design Process
The Problem There is a significant gap between written and listening/speaking abilities of English teachers in Myanmar as they lack sufficient oral training. Sending trainers is costly, time consuming and resource intensive.
The User Research
On-Site Surveys in Myanmar
Personas
User Testings
Results 1
Results 2
Results 3
Key Findings
Insight #1
Teachers find it difficult to download lessons to their phone because the internet connection in Myanmar in is not fast.
Implementation: Divided the app in to different parts - bulk saving per lesson level instead of per grade
Insight #2
Exercises are not interesting enough and needs to be more interactive.
Implementation: Other than speech recognition that shows what the teachers are saying, it would also show the percentage accuracy to the phrase.
Insight #3
The navigation is difficult as it is all in English. Myanmar teachers have a hard time understanding where the button will link them to.
Implementation: Worked with Myanmar teachers to restructure the application’s navigation and redesigned to fit the translated Burmese texts.

Low Fidelity
High Fidelity
Final Prototype


Key Takeaways When we went to Myanmar for User Testing, we realised that we needed to explain on how to use the application and during user testing, it wasn't ideal for us to explain to them as the results on how well they could reach the goal would be inaccurate. On the second day, when we had anotehr group of user testers, we decided not to give to much help and we realised that the problem is that they were overwhelmed with the survey document given to them which were all written in English. From this, we learned that in future test environments, we would need to easily communicate to them the goals of this tests and what kind of feedbacks are trying to get.
